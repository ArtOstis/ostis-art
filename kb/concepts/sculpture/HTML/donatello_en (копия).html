Donato di Niccolò di Betto Bardi

Donatello (Italian. Donatello, full name - Donato di Niccolò di Betto Bardi (Italian: Donato di Niccolò di Betto Bardi); ca. 1386 - December 13, 1466, Florence) - Italian Renaissance sculptor, founder of an individualized sculptural portrait. Donatello was born in Florence (or close to her), around 1386 in the wool combing family, studied in the workshop of the painter and sculptor Bicci di Lorenzo, taking advantage of the patronage of the rich Florentine banker Martelli. To finish his art education in 1404-1407 he traveled to Rome, together with the famous architect Brunelleschi. One of the first works of Donatello was a high relief depicting the Annunciation, made of fine-grained stone.
